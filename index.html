How you get financed

Four steps from application to a running solar system on your roof.

1
Pick your system size
Tell us about your home or business — how many rooms, what appliances, your current monthly spend on power. We'll recommend the right system and show you an estimated monthly payment.
2
Your lender approves the loan
We send your application to our partner bank or MFB. They review your details, run their own credit check, and approve (or decline) the loan. SolarGuard has no say in this — it's the bank's decision, fully.
3
A certified installer visits you
We assign a nearby SolarGuard-certified installer. They survey your site, install the system to professional standards, activate the live IoT monitor, and walk you through the app.
4
Pay monthly — track everything live
Monthly repayments go to your lender by direct debit. Meanwhile, watch your system run in the SolarGuard app. No generator. No fuel runs. No NEPA excuses.

Why SolarGuard

Built for how
Nigeria actually works

Not a fintech that bolted on solar. Not a solar company that built a payments app. Purpose-built infrastructure that de-risks solar lending for all three parties.

📡
Live IoT on every loan
The SGM-100 monitor is non-negotiable on every financed install. Banks see system performance in real time — which makes solar loans less risky to approve.
🛡️
Performance Protection
If your system goes offline for 24+ hours through a fault — not your fault — SolarGuard automatically tells your lender. Many will pause your repayment until it's fixed.
💰
3-tranche disbursement
Installers are paid in three verified milestones. Banks only release funds when work is confirmed. No ghost installations. No unpaid contractors. Everyone is protected.

SLA-scored installer network

Open to all installers
